Team New Zealand (New Zealand National Cricket Team)

1. Overview

  • Governing Body: New Zealand Cricket (NZC).
  • Team Nickname: Black Caps.
  • Captain (Test): Tim Southee.
  • Captain (ODI & T20I): Kane Williamson (ODI), Tim Southee (T20I).
  • Head Coach: Gary Stead.
  • Test Status: Achieved in 1930.
  • ICC Member: Full Member since 1926.

2. Achievements

  • ICC Cricket World Cup:
    • Runners-Up: 2 (2015, 2019).
    • Known for their dramatic finish in the 2019 final against England, which ended in a Super Over.
  • ICC T20 World Cup:
    • Runners-Up: 1 (2021).
  • ICC World Test Championship:
    • Champions: 1 (2021, defeated India in the final).
  • Champions Trophy:
    • Winners: 1 (2000).
      •

3. Key Formats and Rankings (as of 2024):

  • Test Cricket: Known for their resilience and adaptability, especially in home conditions.
  • ODI Cricket: Consistently among the top teams, with a strong track record in World Cup tournaments.
  • T20I Cricket: Known for their aggressive and innovative play in the shortest format.

4. Iconic Players (Past & Present):

  • Legends:
    • Sir Richard Hadlee, Martin Crowe, Daniel Vettori, Brendon McCullum, Stephen Fleming.
  • Current Stars:
    • Kane Williamson, Trent Boult, Tim Southee, Devon Conway, Mitchell Santner.

5. Home Grounds:

  • Major Stadiums:
    • Eden Park.
    • Auckland Hagley Oval.
    • Christchurch Basin Reserve.
    • Wellington Seddon Park.
    • Hamilton University Oval.

6. Team Jersey

  • Test Matches: Traditional white kit with the silver fern logo.
  • ODI & T20I Matches: Predominantly black kit with shades of blue or teal, symbolizing their nickname "Black Caps".
