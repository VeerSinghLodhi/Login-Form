Team India (Indian National Cricket Team)

  1. Overview
    2.
    • Governing Body: Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI ).
    • Team Nickname: Men in Blue.
    • Captain (ODI & T20I): Rohit Sharma.
    • Captain (Test): Rohit Sharma.
    • Head Coach: Gautam Gambhir.
    • ICC Member: Full Member since 1926.
  2. Achievments
    • ICC Cricket World Cup Wins (1983,2011).
    • ICC T20 World Cup Wins (2007,2024).
    • ICC Champions Trophy Wins: 2 (2002* - shared with Sri Lanka, 2013).
    • Asia Cup Wins: 8 (7 in ODI format and 1 in T20 format).
  3. Key Formats and Rankings (as of 2024):
    • Test Cricket: Consistently ranked among the top Test teams, known for their strong batting lineup and spin-friendly home conditions.
    • ODI Cricket: Dominant force with explosive batsmen and quality bowlers, particularly effective in ICC tournaments.
    • T20I Cricket: Known for their aggressive approach and depth in batting and bowling, always a strong contender in global tournaments.
  4. Iconic Players (Past & Present )
    • Legends:
      • Sachin Tendulkar, Kapil Dev, Sunil Gavaskar, Rahul Dravid, Anil Kumble, MS Dhoni, Sourav Ganguly, Virender Sehwag.
    • Current Stars:
      • Virat Kohli, Rohit Sharma, Jasprit Bumrah, Ravindra Jadeja, Mohammed Siraj, Hardik Pandya.
  5. Home Grounds
    • Major Stadiums:
      • Wankhede Stadium.
      • Mumbai Eden Gardens.
      • Kolkata M. Chinnaswamy Stadium.
      • Bengaluru Arun Jaitley Stadium.
      • Delhi Narendra Modi Stadium.
      • Ahmedabad (largest cricket stadium in the world).
      • M.A. Chidambaram Stadium.
  6. Team Jersey
    • Test Matches: Traditional white kit with the BCCI crest.
    • ODI Matches: Iconic blue kit with shades of navy, symbolizing the "Men in Blue".
    • T20I Matches: Modern designs featuring vibrant blue with additional patterns and colors.
  7. Ket Records
    • Highest Team Total in Tests: 759/7 declared against England in 2016.
    • Highest Team Total in ODIs: 418/5 against West Indies in 2011.
    • Most Runs in Tests: Sachin Tendulkar (15,921 runs).
    • Most Runs in ODIs: Sachin Tendulkar (18,426 runs).
    • Most Wickets in Tests: Anil Kumble (619 wickets).
    • Most Wickets in ODIs: Anil Kumble (337 wickets).
